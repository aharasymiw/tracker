Private by architecture, not by promise

There is no server to trust — the app is static files, and your data lives encrypted in your own browser. We couldn’t read it if we wanted to.

  • Encrypted on your device — AES-256-GCM at rest, key derived from your password (PBKDF2, 600k iterations)
  • No accounts, no analytics, no sync — nothing identifies you, nothing phones home
  • Zero third-party requests — browser-enforced by a strict Content-Security-Policy
  • You hold the backups — export JSON (optionally password-encrypted) or CSV, and move devices by moving a file
  • Open source — read the code on GitHub
  • Lost password = lost data — by design; nobody can reset what nobody else holds

Questions, answered

Is my data really private?

Yes — structurally. Less Lately has no backend: the site you load is static files, and every record is encrypted in your browser’s local storage with a key derived from your password. A strict Content-Security-Policy means the app can’t talk to any third party, and the code is open source so you can verify all of it.

What does it cost?

It’s free. No subscription, no premium tier, no ads — and nothing to sell, because we hold none of your data.

Do I need an account?

No. You create a password that encrypts a private vault on your device. There’s no email, no profile, and nothing stored anywhere else.

What happens if I lose my device or password?

Your data lives only on your device, so a lost device — or a forgotten password — means lost data. The app nudges you to export backups (optionally password-encrypted) so you always hold a copy you control.

Is this medical advice?

No. Less Lately is a self-tracking tool for people who want to understand and change their own habits. For health concerns, talk to a professional.
